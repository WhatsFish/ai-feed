# AI Feed Digest — 2026-05-06

_Run at 17:49 — first run of the day_

## Headline

Anthropic bought compute from xAI/SpaceX, and the rest of today's noise — GPT-5.5 Instant, Code w/ Claude announcements — fades next to one rival selling another rival the silicon to keep training Claude.

## Top developments

**Anthropic's compute deal with xAI/SpaceX** ([Anthropic post](https://www.anthropic.com/news/higher-limits-spacex), [xAI post](https://x.ai/news/anthropic-compute-partnership)). Wrap your head around this: the company building Claude is renting capacity from the company building Grok, on infrastructure tied to SpaceX. The "higher usage limits" framing is corporate decoration — the real story is that compute scarcity is now severe enough that frontier labs are willing to fund competitors' data centers to get power-shovels in the ground. Read this alongside OpenAI's Stargate announcements and you have the actual 2026 AI story: capex, not capabilities.

**OpenAI's MRC networking protocol, now confirmed as Nvidia Spectrum-X MRC** ([OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/mrc-supercomputer-networking), [STH coverage](https://www.servethehome.com/nvidia-spectrum-x-mrc-is-the-custom-rdma-transport-protocol-for-gigascale-ai/)). OpenAI open-sourced their training-cluster RDMA protocol via OCP — and ServeTheHome immediately pointed out it's the custom transport Nvidia has been quietly shipping in Spectrum-X. This is the kind of plumbing announcement that gets dismissed but matters: the post-InfiniBand consensus on how to wire 100k-GPU clusters is settling, and it's settling on something not-RoCE.

**Code w/ Claude 2026: dreaming, outcomes, multiagent orchestration** ([Claude blog](https://claude.com/blog/new-in-claude-managed-agents), [Ars](https://arstechnica.com/ai/2026/05/anthropics-claude-can-now-dream-sort-of/), [Simon Willison live blog](https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/6/code-w-claude-2026/)). Anthropic is naming their memory-consolidation step "dreaming," which is mostly marketing for offline summarization. The substantive bit is "outcomes" — agents getting graded on goal-completion rather than step-correctness, which is the right direction the industry has been circling for a year. Worth tracking whether the orchestration primitives become something other shops can target.

**GPT-5.5 Instant ships as ChatGPT default** ([blog](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-instant), [system card](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-instant-system-card)). Quiet update, but it's the model touching most of OpenAI's daily traffic. The interesting context is the [Codex base_instructions leak](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/28/openai-codex/) ("never talk about goblins, gremlins, raccoons…") — they're still patching the personality regressions documented in [Where the goblins came from](https://openai.com/index/where-the-goblins-came-from). Production prompt-engineering on a model serving hundreds of millions is a brittle business.

**MolmoAct2 / VLAs going open** ([HF paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.02881), 195 upvotes). Open-weight vision-language-action model for robotics that doesn't require expensive proprietary hardware. Worth flagging because robotics has been the last subfield where open models lagged hopelessly behind closed; this is the first credible push.

## Themes

**The post-benchmark era is here.** Two unrelated sources said the same thing this week: Latent Space's "Tasteful Tokenmaxxing" and Nathan Lambert's "Reading today's open-closed performance gap." Single-number benchmarks are now table stakes; what differentiates models is taste, character, agentic reliability — things that don't reduce to a leaderboard score. Sebastian Raschka quietly making the same point with his [4-approach evaluation framework](https://magazine.sebastianraschka.com/p/llm-evaluation-4-approaches).

**Liability is catching up.** The Canadian fiddler suing Google over an AI Overview false sex-offender claim, Apple settling Siri privacy ($25–95 per user), the LA Times piece on Israel's phone-data targeting system. The defamation/harm lawsuits are no longer hypothetical; they're settling.
