# AI Feed — 2026-05-06

_Run 1 — 18:00 Asia/Shanghai_

## Headline
Anthropic is buying compute from xAI and SpaceX — the three companies most loudly competing for AGI are now openly trading the infrastructure none of them can build fast enough alone.

## Top developments

**1. Anthropic ↔ xAI/SpaceX compute deal.** Anthropic announced higher Claude usage limits backed by capacity from xAI and SpaceX ([Anthropic post](https://www.anthropic.com/news/higher-limits-spacex), [x.ai counterpart](https://x.ai/news/anthropic-compute-partnership), 137 points on HN within hours). Read past the press-release framing: a year ago "Claude is buying compute from the company that makes Grok" would have been a contradiction. It isn't anymore. Compute has decoupled from ideology, and frontier labs are now reduced to landlords renting each other's GPUs. This is the most economically revealing AI story of 2026 so far.

**2. OpenAI publishes MRC, a new RDMA transport, via OCP.** [MRC (Multipath Reliable Connection)](https://openai.com/index/mrc-supercomputer-networking) is OpenAI's networking protocol for gigascale training, contributed to the Open Compute Project. STH [confirms it is the Spectrum-X custom transport](https://www.servethehome.com/nvidia-spectrum-x-mrc-is-the-custom-rdma-transport-protocol-for-gigascale-ai/) — i.e., OpenAI co-designed the wire protocol for Nvidia's flagship AI fabric. Pair this with the [Stargate expansion post](https://openai.com/index/building-the-compute-infrastructure-for-the-intelligence-age) and the message is clear: OpenAI is positioning itself as a networking-and-datacenter company that happens to ship models.

**3. GPT-5.5 Instant ships as the new ChatGPT default.** [Launch](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-instant) plus [system card](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-instant-system-card). The framing — "smarter, clearer, more personalized" — is unremarkable; what matters is cadence. OpenAI shipped GPT-5.5 in April, GPT-5.5 Instant two weeks later, and the bio bug bounty in parallel. The model layer has gone from event releases to a steady drip of point upgrades. Benchmarks are no longer the news; deployment friction is.

**4. Code w/ Claude 2026 — Claude Managed Agents get "dreaming."** Anthropic's dev event today introduced [multiagent orchestration, outcomes, and a "dreaming" memory-consolidation step](https://claude.com/blog/new-in-claude-managed-agents) ([Ars coverage](https://arstechnica.com/ai/2026/05/anthropics-claude-can-now-dream-sort-of/), [Simon Willison's live blog](https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/6/code-w-claude-2026/)). Strip the marketing word — "dreaming" is offline replay/consolidation between sessions, the obvious next step once agents accumulate enough state to need garbage collection. Useful, not mystical.

**5. "Vibe Physics" — GPT-5.x derives new results in quantum gravity.** Alex Lupsasca (OpenAI) walks through how GPT-5.x produced novel theoretical-physics results ([Latent Space](https://www.latent.space/p/lupsasca)). Take the claim seriously but read the methodology: "model-discovered" results in math/physics still depend on human verification of every step. This is the strongest concrete data point yet that frontier models can contribute to research-grade theory work, and worth more than another agent benchmark.

## Themes

**Compute is the moat — and it's a rented one.** Anthropic↔xAI, OpenAI's MRC contribution, and Stargate's continued expansion all converge: model differentiation is collapsing, but no one has enough fabric. Expect more cross-rival compute swaps before they stop being newsworthy.

**Agents are the deployment surface, not chat.** ChatGPT [workspace agents](https://openai.com/index/introducing-workspace-agents-in-chatgpt), Codex's [Symphony orchestration spec](https://openai.com/index/open-source-codex-orchestration-symphony), Claude Managed Agents, and HuggingFace's top paper [OpenSeeker-v2](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.04036) all treat the agent loop as the unit of product, not the chatbox. The 2024 chat metaphor is being retired in real time.

## Worth reading in full
- [Anthropic ↔ xAI/SpaceX compute partnership](https://www.anthropic.com/news/higher-limits-spacex) — the year's most revealing industry story, hidden inside a usage-limit press release.
- [Vibe Physics with Alex Lupsasca](https://www.latent.space/p/lupsasca) — the most substantive "AI does science" claim of the week, and an actual walkthrough rather than a press release.
- [MolmoAct2 (HuggingFace #1, 195 upvotes)](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.02881) — open-weight VLA model targeting cheap real-world hardware; the open-vs-closed gap in robotics is starting to look defensible.

## Skipped as noise
Dropped: the dozens of Codex documentation/academy pages, every customer-success post (Choco, Hyatt, PwC, Shopify), ChatGPT Ads Manager (an ad product, not an AI story), the Apple Siri settlement (legal cleanup, not new ground), most arXiv submissions (sentiment analysis, Indic ASR, autism intervention agents — fine work, not frontier-shifting), and the long tail of HN show-HN agent vaults. The Chrome-downloads-4GB-AI-file story is interesting but unverified.

_Run 2 — 19:26 Asia/Shanghai_

## Headline
Two hours after run 1, the cadence confirms itself: OpenAI is shipping enterprise plumbing — Agents SDK runtime, Cloudflare distribution, a cyber-tier model — while the financial press starts pricing AI as a debt story rather than a capability one.

## Top developments

**1. OpenAI Agents SDK gets native sandbox execution and a model-native harness.** The [next evolution of the Agents SDK](https://openai.com/index/the-next-evolution-of-the-agents-sdk) folds the runtime into the SDK: sandboxed tool calls, a harness the models were trained against, long-running file/tool state. This is the SDK becoming the runtime — OpenAI isn't shipping an API anymore, it's shipping the substrate. Combined with the [Symphony orchestration spec](https://openai.com/index/open-source-codex-orchestration-symphony) from earlier today, the pattern is clear: lock down the agent execution layer.

**2. GPT-5.4-Cyber lands inside Trusted Access for Cyber.** OpenAI [expanded the Trusted Access program](https://openai.com/index/scaling-trusted-access-for-cyber-defense) and gated GPT-5.4-Cyber behind it — vetted defenders only. Tiered model access for safety-sensitive domains (cyber, bio) is becoming the actual product surface for advanced capabilities, not the public API. The implication: capability disclosures will increasingly happen inside customer programs, not press releases.

**3. Cloudflare brings OpenAI to Agent Cloud.** GPT-5.4 and Codex are now [available inside Cloudflare's Agent Cloud](https://openai.com/index/cloudflare-openai-agent-cloud). Cloudflare's pitch is that the agent runtime sits at the edge, not at OpenAI's datacenter. Pair this with [OpenAI's AWS landing](https://openai.com/index/openai-on-aws) (run 1) and OpenAI is no longer demanding traffic come to it — the model goes wherever the customer's compute already is.

**4. Invesco: "Big Tech needs every dollar it can get in AI debt."** [Bloomberg via HN.](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-06/invesco-says-big-tech-needs-every-dollar-it-can-get-in-ai-debt) The most quotable framing of run 1's compute-deal theme: the financial market is treating AI infrastructure as a capex/debt story. Anthropic↔xAI, Stargate, MRC — institutional investors aren't betting on capabilities, they're underwriting the buildout. The competitive frontier is balance-sheet capacity, not benchmarks.

## Themes

**The product is the runtime, not the model.** Agents SDK with native sandboxing, Symphony orchestration spec, GPT-5.4-Cyber gated through Trusted Access, Cloudflare Agent Cloud — none of these are model news. They're substrate news. Every shipping announcement from OpenAI today is about controlling where and how agents execute, which is exactly where the recurring revenue lives.

**Capex, not capabilities.** Anthropic↔xAI in run 1, Invesco's debt comment in run 2, Stargate and MRC running parallel — across editorial sources, the AI story is being re-framed as an infrastructure financing story. Expect more bond issuances and compute swaps before more leaderboard wins.

## Worth reading in full
- [OpenAI Agents SDK update](https://openai.com/index/the-next-evolution-of-the-agents-sdk) — substrate-layer change, not a feature drop. Read for the architecture, not the marketing.
- [Invesco / AI debt (Bloomberg)](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-06/invesco-says-big-tech-needs-every-dollar-it-can-get-in-ai-debt) — the cleanest external framing of where AI margins are actually flowing.
- [PhysicianBench](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.02240) — agent evaluation grounded in real EHR data instead of synthetic clinical scaffolding; the harder kind of benchmark.

Eight more OpenAI Academy pages (Financial Services, Sales, Customer Success, Image Generation) — pure docs. HN noise: AI-video clippers, agent logging proxies, the inevitable "exclude AI from Show HN" meta thread, an "AI Architecture Governance for Governments" landing page. _Run 3 — 19:30 Asia/Shanghai_

## Headline
A thin third run: Amazon's Andy Jassy joins the AI-capex justification tour, and the day's real news is already in runs 1 and 2.

## Top developments

**1. Andy Jassy publicly defends Amazon's AI capex to investors.** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/04/andy-jassy-amazon-investors-rewarded-ai-spending.html), surfaced via HN. Same beat as Invesco from run 2, but now it's a CEO making the pitch instead of an asset manager. Amazon joining Anthropic, OpenAI, and Microsoft in the "trust us, the spend is rational" chorus is the tell: every hyperscaler now needs an investor-relations narrative for AI capex, because the numbers no longer fit a normal margin model.

**2. RL for LLM-based Multi-Agent Systems through Orchestration Traces.** [HuggingFace paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.02801) that lands directly on top of today's Symphony / Agents SDK news: instead of optimizing individual agent actions, train RL policies over the spawn/delegate/communicate/aggregate/stop graph. This is the academic version of what OpenAI shipped commercially this morning — the orchestration trace, not the token, is the unit of optimization. The first credible attempt to formalize what a "good" agentic loop looks like.

**3. A Grand Challenge for Reliable Coding in the Age of AI Agents.** [arXiv perspective piece](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17150), low HN signal so far, but the framing is correct: the Codex/Claude Code shipping cadence is dramatically outpacing any rigorous notion of correctness for AI-written code. Worth flagging as the counter-narrative to today's runtime/SDK marketing — the substrate is consolidating faster than the safety story.

## Themes

**AI capex now needs an investor narrative.** Invesco in run 2, Jassy in run 3. Two days in a row, the public framing of AI is shifting from product to balance sheet. CFOs and CEOs are now expected to justify AI spend the way they once justified data-center buildouts — and the rebuttals are starting to lag the spend.

## Worth reading in full
- [RL through Orchestration Traces](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.02801) — the right object for RL on agents is the orchestration trace, not individual tool calls. First clean academic statement of what OpenAI shipped commercially today.
